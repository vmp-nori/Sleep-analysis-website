Mental Health & Stress

Emotional resilience is heavily dependent on allowing the brain to recover optimally.

Emotional Stability Curve

Emotional stability follows the same optimal range (7-9 hours). With proper sleep, the brain successfully processes memories and handles stress.

Sleep Loss (<6h)

Impairs emotional regulation, leading to excessive reactions to negative stimuli and heightened daily stress.

Excess Sleep (>9h)

Lethargy takes over, resulting in groggy awakenings and sluggish mental states that compound feelings of depression.

Caffeine Cycle

Self-medication through caffeine increases in low-sleep individuals (avg 1.5+ cups/day vs <2 cups for well-rested), which feeds into a cycle of poor sleep latency.
