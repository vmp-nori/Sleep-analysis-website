Academic Performance

Productivity strictly follows an inverse U-shaped pattern, peaking at 8 hours of sleep.

Productivity Score vs Sleep

The Impact on Brain Function

Based on massive datasets (54,102 students) and JMSS survey productivity scores, cognitive functions such as memory consolidation, problem-solving, and sustained focus peak significantly at the 7-9 hour range.

~25%

Of students average 7 hours or less on school days.

Drop-offs

Both insufficient (<6h) and excessive (>9h) sleep reduce productivity.

The drop-off after 9 hours is potentially due to oversleeping causing students to be late or groggy, missing crucial study periods.
