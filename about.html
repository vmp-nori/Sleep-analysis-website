We paired Calistoga and Outfit to strike a balance between approachability and modern analytical precision. Calistoga's soft, rounded serifs lend a friendly, story-book feel to our large headers, making the dense subject matter feel welcoming. In contrast, Outfit is a geometrical sans-serif that provides crisp legibility for our data-heavy body paragraphs and UI components.

Physical Activity vs Sleep

Graph Analysis

This bar chart demonstrates the relationship between the distance travelled (physical output) by JMSS students against the hours they slept. Notice the peak distribution occurring right around the 7-8 hour mark.

The sharp drop-off in physical activity for sleep-deprived students (<6 hours) is attributed to poor muscle recovery and reduced daytime stamina, increasing lethargy and injury risk. Conversely, while students sleeping >9 hours might be fully rested, the data shows they travel significantly less distance. This is primarily because oversleeping encroaches on the available daytime hours, leaving fewer opportunities for physical activity, or indicating underlying chronic fatigue. The 7-9 hour range is the optimal "sweet spot" supporting both muscle repair and sufficient waking hours for an active lifestyle.
